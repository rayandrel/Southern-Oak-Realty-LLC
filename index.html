Operating Expenses

Deductible costs for Schedule E

📌 Instructions — IRS Schedule E Deductible Expenses:
  • Repairs vs. Improvements: Repairs (fix a leak, patch drywall) are deductible immediately. Improvements that add value or extend useful life must be capitalized and depreciated — log those under Refurbishment.
  • Mortgage interest is deductible; principal payments are NOT.
  • Property management fees, insurance, property taxes, utilities, advertising — all deductible.
  • Home office / vehicle mileage for rental management may be deductible — keep a mileage log.
  • Keep all receipts. The IRS recommends records for 3–7 years.

Refurbishment & Capital Improvements

Track property upgrades and add to depreciable basis

📌 Instructions — Capital Improvements vs. Repairs:
  • Capital Improvements (must be depreciated): new roof, HVAC system, kitchen remodel, addition, new flooring throughout — anything that materially adds value, extends life, or adapts property to new use.
  • Safe Harbor de minimis rule (IRS): Items costing $2,500 or less per invoice may be deducted immediately if you have an applicable financial statement, or $2,500 otherwise (Rev. Proc. 2015-20).
  • Routine maintenance (repairs): repainting, fixing broken fixtures, replacing a single appliance under $2,500 — deduct on Schedule E.
  • Capital improvements add to your property's cost basis, which increases depreciation deductions over time and reduces capital gains when you sell.

Depreciation Schedule

MACRS depreciation for rental properties (Form 4562)

📌 How Depreciation Works for Rental LLCs:
  • Residential rental property: Depreciated over 27.5 years using the straight-line MACRS method (GDS). Reported on Schedule E / Form 4562.
  • Commercial property: Depreciated over 39 years.
  • Depreciable basis = Purchase price − Land value + Capital improvements. Land is NEVER depreciated.
  • Mid-month convention: In the first and last year, you only get a half-month's depreciation for the month placed in service.
  • Bonus Depreciation / Section 179: May apply to personal property (appliances, carpeting) placed in service — consult your CPA for current-year rules.
  • Depreciation recapture (25% tax rate) applies when you sell — track it carefully.

Tax Estimator

Federal & State estimated tax liability

📌 How Rental LLC Taxes Work:
  • A single-member LLC is a disregarded entity — rental income flows to your personal return (Schedule E, Form 1040). The LLC itself pays no federal income tax.
  • A multi-member LLC files Form 1065 and issues K-1s to each member.
  • Rental income is generally NOT subject to self-employment tax (unlike active business income) — a key tax advantage.
  • Passive Activity Loss (PAL) rules: Rental losses can offset rental income. If your Adjusted Gross Income is under $100,000 and you actively participate, you can deduct up to $25,000 of rental losses against other income.
  • Quarterly estimated taxes: If you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal tax, pay quarterly (Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15).
  • This tool provides estimates only. Consult a CPA for your actual tax return.

LLC & Company Setup

Business information and compliance checklist

📌 LLC Setup Requirements for Rental Property Companies:
  • EIN (Employer Identification Number): Required for multi-member LLCs; recommended for single-member LLCs. Apply free at IRS.gov.
  • Separate bank account: Always keep LLC finances 100% separate from personal funds. Commingling can pierce the corporate veil.
  • Operating Agreement: Legally required in many states; always advisable. Defines ownership, profit sharing, and management.
  • Annual Report / Franchise Tax: Most states require an annual LLC filing and fee. Due dates vary by state.
  • Registered Agent: Required in all states — a person or company designated to receive legal documents.
